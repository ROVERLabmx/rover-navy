Delivered Brand strategy 2022—2023

Navy Training Center

"We were growing, but something felt off." A regional fitness brand had reached the edge of what informal could carry. We helped them build the system underneath.

The problem

Navy had grown into a recognizable Mexican fitness brand with a loyal community across multiple studios. The team wanted to expand into new states, but everything that worked at one location had been built informally — communication, pricing, product, look-and-feel. Without a system, expansion meant either inconsistency or paralysis. The brand needed to become reproducible without losing what made it work in the first place.

The mechanism

01

Diagnose

Audited every customer-facing layer — location, product, pricing, communication — against the brand's real personality and the premium fitness market.

02

Define

Set positioning, language, and product architecture that could survive distance — same brand, new staff, new city.

03

Design

Built the system: new product line, tiered pricing, evergreen content, staff manuals, visual and verbal guidelines.

04

Deploy

Rolled out across existing locations and prepared the foundation for franchise expansion.

What we built

Brand strategy + visual and verbal guidelines
The Galley — smoothie bar product line
Tiered pricing strategy across services
Product naming system across categories
Staff manuals and operations playbooks
Evergreen content strategy + calendar

The shift

Before

A successful single-location feel that didn't transfer cleanly to new staff or new cities.

After

A brand-as-system. Reproducible, teachable, ready to franchise.
