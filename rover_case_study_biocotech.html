Ongoing Brand strategy Market entry 2023—Present

Biocotech Americas

"We had the technology. We couldn't get buyers to hear what it does." A composting technology company entering the North American market needed its brand to catch up to its capability. The work is in progress.

The problem

Biocotech Americas builds industrial composting systems — proven, sophisticated, sustainable. But the brand they had carried in had been built for a different market, a different scale, and a different audience. Entering the US and Canadian B2B market meant facing established players, more skeptical buyers, and a sales cycle that punishes any signal of "we're not quite ready." Their language, their materials, and their identity needed to reflect what BCTA had become — not what it had started as.

The mechanism

01

Diagnose

Audited the existing brand against the North American competitive set — who BCTA was up against, how they spoke, where the language gap actually lived.

02

Define

Reframed positioning around concrete impact and operational seriousness. Rebuilt brand foundation, aspiration, and promise to carry both sustainability and industrial credibility.

03

Design

New visual identity, brand voice, sales-team materials, complementary service offerings, technical whitepapers — design system in collaboration with Ricky Ramos Studio.

04

Deploy In progress

Rolling out new sales materials and website language. Iterating based on what's actually landing in active buyer conversations.

What we've built so far

Brand strategy and positioning for North America
New brand identity system (with Ricky Ramos Studio)
Sales-team support materials across 3 angles
BioSpeed Maintenance — productized service line
Whitepaper: Golden Gate Park BioSpeed integration
Website redesign and language download

The shift

Before

A capable industrial company speaking in language built for a different scale and a different audience.

In progress

A brand whose voice, materials, and identity match the maturity of the technology — and the seriousness of the buyers it's now reaching.

Where we are now

The new brand foundation, identity, and first wave of sales materials are in market. The website redesign is in deployment. We're tracking how the language is landing in active buyer conversations and iterating on the materials based on what's earning second meetings and what isn't.

Next phases: category-education content for procurement audiences, partner program structure, and a pricing strategy for the BioSpeed Maintenance service line.

In B2B, the brand is permission. If the language doesn't earn it, the technology never gets evaluated.
